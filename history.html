Chronicles of the Night

From pixelated beginnings to gothic masterpieces, explore the key milestones that shaped the Castlevania legacy.

1986: The Beginning

The original Castlevania debuts on the NES, introducing Simon Belmont and Dracula’s cursed castle. Players follow Simon as he navigates treacherous halls filled with monsters, traps, and supernatural foes, all in pursuit of the vampire lord. Along the way, he must master his trusty whip and uncover hidden secrets to survive. The game’s dark gothic atmosphere and challenging gameplay set the stage for a legendary franchise.

1997: Symphony of the Night

Alucard’s adventure helped shape the “Metroidvania” style, combining exploration, combat, and discovery. In Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, players explore a vast, interconnected castle full of hidden rooms, powerful upgrades, and secret paths that reward careful exploration. Its mix of action, RPG mechanics, and backtracking set a lasting standard for non-linear adventure games, inspiring countless titles that followed.

2010: Lords of Shadow

A cinematic reimagining that weaves myth and tragedy into the timeless struggle between light and darkness. It follows heroes and villains whose choices shape the fate of the world, with every battle carrying high stakes. Rich visuals and immersive storytelling bring this epic tale to life, exploring themes of sacrifice, destiny, and the enduring fight between good and evil.
