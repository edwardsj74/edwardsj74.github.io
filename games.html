From haunted castles to cursed bloodlines, the Castlevania series has delivered unforgettable adventures across generations. This gallery showcases some of the most iconic entries from handheld classics to animated epics—that have shaped the franchise’s gothic legacy. Prepare to meet heroes, monsters, and magic as you explore the games that defined the night.

Portrait of Ruin (2006)

Join Jonathan and Charlotte as they journey through magical portraits to prevent Dracula’s resurrection. This DS classic combines dual-character gameplay with eerie, immersive environments and deep, engaging lore. Players switch between the two heroes to solve puzzles, fight challenging enemies, and unravel the mysteries of Dracula’s dark world, making it a memorable and atmospheric entry in the Castlevania series.

Order of Ecclesia (2008)

In this fast-paced DS entry, Shanoa wields powerful glyph magic in a game celebrated for its challenge and design. Set against a backdrop of gothic decay, the story explores themes of sacrifice and power. Players harness Shanoa’s glyphs to absorb enemy abilities, solve puzzles, and defeat fearsome foes while exploring a sprawling, secret-filled castle. With its atmospheric visuals, intricate level design, and haunting soundtrack, the game offers a deeply immersive Castlevania experience.

Netflix Series (2017–2021)

An animated retelling blending lore, violence, and emotion. With stunning visuals and complex characters, the series keeps the legacy alive for a new generation of fans. It expands on the classic tale with rich storytelling, exploring the human side of heroes and monsters alike. Through its powerful dialogue, striking animation, and emotional depth, the series redefines Castlevania for the modern era while honoring its gothic roots.
